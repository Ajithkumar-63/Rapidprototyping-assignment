        <section class="about">
  <h2>Design Sprint - Rapid Prototyping</h2>
  <p>Design Sprint develops key skill areas essential for a product designer from the perspective of design, its inherent complexity and support them with tools, techniques to prototype rapidly. The hands-on activities during the course are organised to enable the participants to visualise the experience for a user, and learn the roles , responsibilities of a designer in creating and shapping experiences for the user. The participants shall learn through the lenses of system thinking of how existing products work and become capable of selecting , applying various practice tools that will aid them in rapid prototyping.</p>

<p>Upon the successful completion of the course, the students will be able to :
<br>1 Understand the elements and principle of design in the context of product and service design
<br>2 Apply user research techniques to meet UX needs of a customer and design a visual prototype
<br>3 Apply system thinking to reverse engineer a prototype and understand its internal components and their correlations
<br>4 Use tools to create  mechanical, electrical and SW prototypes in a quick iterative methodology </p>
</section>

  <h2>Design Sprint - Rapid Prototyping</h2>
  <p>Design Sprint develops key skill areas essential for a product designer from the perspective of design, its inherent complexity and support them with tools, techniques to prototype rapidly. The hands-on activities during the course are organised to enable the participants to visualise the experience for a user, and learn the roles , responsibilities of a designer in creating and shapping experiences for the user. The participants shall learn through the lenses of system thinking of how existing products work and become capable of selecting , applying various practice tools that will aid them in rapid prototyping.</p>

<p>Upon the successful completion of the course, the students will be able to :
<br>1 Understand the elements and principle of design in the context of product and service design
<br>2 Apply user research techniques to meet UX needs of a customer and design a visual prototype
<br>3 Apply system thinking to reverse engineer a prototype and understand its internal components and their correlations
<br>4 Use tools to create  mechanical, electrical and SW prototypes in a quick iterative methodology </p>

        <section class="about">
  <h2>Design Sprint - Rapid Prototyping</h2>
  <p>Design Sprint develops key skill areas essential for a product designer from the perspective of design, its inherent complexity and support them with tools, techniques to prototype rapidly. The hands-on activities during the course are organised to enable the participants to visualise the experience for a user, and learn the roles , responsibilities of a designer in creating and shapping experiences for the user. The participants shall learn through the lenses of system thinking of how existing products work and become capable of selecting , applying various practice tools that will aid them in rapid prototyping.</p>

<p>Upon the successful completion of the course, the students will be able to :
<br>1 Understand the elements and principle of design in the context of product and service design
<br>2 Apply user research techniques to meet UX needs of a customer and design a visual prototype
<br>3 Apply system thinking to reverse engineer a prototype and understand its internal components and their correlations
<br>4 Use tools to create  mechanical, electrical and SW prototypes in a quick iterative methodology </p>
